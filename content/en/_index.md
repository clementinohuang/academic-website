PhD student in the History of Science, studying nineteenth-century Sino-French encounters in the field of natural history.

Based at York University, Toronto, Canada, I have broad interests in the global cross-pollination between epistemic traditions of science, art, and religion. I am an avid language learner, always curious about the experiences and motivations of beings that moved, or were moved, across borders.

My doctoral research is supported by a [Canada Graduate Research Scholarship – Doctoral (CGRS-D)](https://sshrc-crsh.canada.ca/en/funding/opportunities/canada-graduate-research-scholarships/doctoral-program.aspx) from the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada (SSHRC).

## My Research

My dissertation project, *Redefining Chinoiserie: Chinese–French Encounters in Natural Knowledge, 1830–1870*, examines the bidirectional yet asymmetrical flows of natural knowledge between France and China, entangled with missionary expansion and the treaty-port system. [Read more →](research/)
